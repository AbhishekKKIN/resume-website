What should I add in my resume?

Jerry recruiter at Showflipper

I would like to divide your resume into two parts : Must have sections and ETC sections.

Must have sections in your resume

  • Summary and Objective
  • Contact details, email likewise
  • Profession and educational history
  • Key Achievements

ETC section in your resume

  • Hobbies
  • Extra curricular
  • Awards and recognition
  • Event participation
  • Voluntary work
  • References

When you put extra curricular, hobbies, events etc in your resume you should make an attempt to support your claim in the main section. You might have won a marathon and also an essay writing competition. If you are applying for a content writing post, mentioning about an essay writing competition will be more advantageous than having won a marathon.

Everything in the ETC section has to be specific. It is also perfectly fine if you haven't done any voluntary work or won any award. We should avoid putting it just for the sake of it. The ETC section kind of connects the dots for recruiters. We can get into the psychographics of the candidate.

How to format a resume?

Jasmine at Drap Daily

ATS should be able to track your resume. There is no rocket science. Follow KISS (Keep it Simple, Silly) principal. There is no perfect format however one must stick to following basic guidelines while formating their resumes.

  1. Even margins on all four sides.
  2. Font consistency. Avoid Italics, underlines and too or too small fonts. 11-12-13 are decent and most acceptable font sizes. Verdana, Times New Roman and Arial are considered as good font types. If you are a graphic designer it is perfectly fine if you use font types and sizes other than the standard ones.
  3. Paragraph and line spacing. 1.5 line spacing is fine.
  4. Don't make your resume too lengthy or too small. Have full sentences and also avoid short forms.
  5. Photos are not required. At times they may hurt your chances. Images may make file size heavy and most recruiters don't accept files above 1 MB.

Tricks and Tips: How to Make a Cover Letter

First impression is the last impression. A good professionally written cover letter surely helps in leaving a positive impression about you in the minds of your future employer. There are tonnes of books, blogs and videos on how to make cover sheet templates? You don't need to browse pages after pages to make a perfect cover letter as Print CV practically tells you how to go about it and stand ahead of the race.

Just choose a template - follow our tips - download and start applying. So what makes a good cover letter? Just follow these 5 tips and you can make a perfect cover letter.

What makes you a good candidate?

You need to get into the employer's shoes. 99% of the companies do ask the following question in every interview they conduct.

Why do you want to work with us?

This is precisely something that you can answer in your cover letter. You need to know what job position you are applying for.

Just a couple of lines about why you wish to work with them will work wonders for you.

A simple trick here is, just read the recruitment ad and study the skill sets and responsibilities your employer is looking for. Simply relate yourself to the job you are applying for.

This simple step will increase your chances by 37%. Yes, it's true! Print CV conducted a survey and here is the finding. Click here.

Mind your words

Know your industry well. You need to use industry lingo to make a good cover letter. Most candidates use words which don't really sync well with industry standards in their cover letters.

In a sales cover letter it is perfectly fine to say things like: Go - Getter, Hardworking, hit the ground running etc.

For some other jobs such as software engineer such words may not sync well with the cover letter. One may consider words like discipline, multitasker and detailed oriented etc. as in case of making a cover letter for a software engineer position.

You should be well versed with your industry lingo and make use of those words in your cover letter.

Don't under or over sell

When you are preparing a cover letter just ensure you don't boost off. Some candidates over promise and a few do not talk much about themselves. Don't land up in a situation where you cannot really back up your claims. Similarly don't be shy. Just be realistic.

Write a professional cover letter

Never use short forms or slang while preparing your cover letter. Words like: Kinda, etc, basically

Idioms like:-So far so good, I was like

Abbreviations like: - &, BTW

Make sure you are professional throughout your cover letter. If you use slang the interviewer may feel you are difficult to mould and not a professional candidate. Idioms may make your interviewer believe that you are less sure about everything.

The best cover letter template does not have such rookie mistakes.

Structure your cover letter

The best cover letter generally has four main sections.

What to write in your cover letter?

What to add in the first paragraph of your cover letter: Who you are?

Just put your personal information and greeting to your employer.

-What is the position you are applying for?

- From where did you get information about the job you are applying for?

What to add in the second paragraph of your cover letter: How can we work together?

Simply sell yourself but remember don't over or under sell. Keep a couple of adjectives ready which describe you in true sense and are industry specific. Don't forget the industry lingo here.

Example

I have excellent knowledge about the sales force and I am also a Zend certified engineer. I can understand client's requirements quickly as I am a detail oriented individual.

Now comes the third paragraph of your cover letter: Why this company?

Try to check what company is doing off late and relate this to your career goals. Just don't make it sound very obvious.

Example: On a company's blog page you have found an article they will be working on financial applications along with their current portfolio.

You may write something that may connect the dots. Describe your achievements

I have worked on financial applications as well. I had a very high success ratio and retention rate.

Here you are trying to make a deal based on your past performances, achievements and interests.

If you do not have something very specific which may connect the dots then add some achievements and interests.

If you are a fresher then write university or college level achievements which may match your employer's current job brief.

Fourth paragraph; How to sum up a cover letter? : Thank you, follow up and your personal details.

Here you need to professionally request an interview, leave your professional details and ask the employer about follow up.
